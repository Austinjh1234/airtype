Who it's for

One input, everywhere you can't type.

AirType outputs a standard Bluetooth keyboard, so the same muscle memory works across the places a keyboard can't follow you — headsets, the couch, the bus, or hands that need a different path to text.

01 · XR / AR + VR

Text entry for headsets

Smart glasses and headsets ship with no surface to type on — today you peck at a floating keyboard or talk out loud. With AirType you rest your hands on your lap and type at full speed, privately, while the decoder turns motion into clean QWERTY for the headset over BLE.

02 · ACCESSIBILITY

Assistive input, no relearning

For the 1.3 billion people who need an alternative path to text, most options demand learning a new alphabet or accepting low accuracy. AirType maps the QWERTY movements many already have, adapts per-hand through calibration, and stays an open keyboard to any device.

03 · MOBILE / PRODUCTIVITY

Type anywhere, on anything

Phones and tablets are great to read on and slow to write on. AirType is a full keyboard you wear — answer a long email on the train, write standing up, or drive a TV across the room. It pairs like any Bluetooth keyboard, so every device just works.

04 · DEVELOPERS

An open HID building block

Because AirType speaks standard Bluetooth HID, it needs no app and no driver — and that makes it a clean input primitive to build on. The same calibrated, per-finger signal that types can drive shortcuts, pointer control, or custom mappings.
