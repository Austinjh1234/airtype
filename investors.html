The honest view

The hurdles we're underwriting.

A concept this ambitious has real risk. Here's where it lives — and why we think it's tractable now.

Technical

  • Surfaceless taps have no hard "keystroke" instant to detect.
  • Each finger covers 2–4 keys — ambiguity the decoder must resolve.
  • Drift & per-user variation demand calibration + re-baselining.
  • Latency budget: sub-50 ms, on-device, on a battery.

Regulatory / market

  • Wireless (BLE) certification and per-region compliance.
  • Assistive-tech claims must stay honest and, where needed, validated.
  • A well-funded incumbent (Meta) is validating — and competing.
  • Hardware supply chain and unit economics at scale.

Human factors

  • All-day comfort, fit across hand sizes, washability.
  • Accuracy that's good enough to trust for real writing.
  • Onboarding: calibration must feel near-instant.
  • Earning the switch from a habit as old as the keyboard.
